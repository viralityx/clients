02

Critical Findings — Found Today

Three technical issues caught in live HTML inspection. Each one is silently capping the growth shown above. Fixing them is the unlock.

1
Critical — Indexing Issue

Every page on the site is telling Google to ignore it.

Both /services and /about have a canonical tag pointing to the homepage. This is a hard signal to Google saying "don't index this page — use the homepage instead."

What we found in the live HTML
<link rel="canonical" href="https://www.coral-med.com">  — on every page

Why it matters: Service pages literally cannot rank because Google is being told they're duplicates of the homepage. The growth metrics above are happening DESPITE this issue — fixing it unlocks the entire site's ability to rank for service-specific keywords (weight loss, DPC, primary care, etc.).

Update each page's canonical to point to itself. 15-minute fix in Replit. Unlocks the entire site's ability to rank for service-specific keywords.
2
Critical — Duplicate Meta

Every page has the same title and description.

Live HTML check: homepage, /services, /about all return identical meta titles and descriptions. The SEO meta rewrite we built last month is sitting in a document — never deployed to production.

Current title (on every page)
"Primary Care in Huntington Beach | Coral Med"

Why it matters: This is precisely why "coral med" shows 85 monthly impressions but only 4 clicks (4.7% CTR vs. our 7.1% sitewide average). Each page needs a unique, intent-matched title to earn the click.

Deploy the SEO meta document we already wrote. One push to Replit. Immediately improves CTR on every existing impression — no new traffic required.
3
Critical — NAP Inconsistency

Dr. Moussa is listed in the wrong city on 3 major directories.

Live citation check: Vitals, Healthgrades, and WebMD all list Dr. Moussa as Los Angeles, CA instead of Huntington Beach. Only Doximity and USNews show the correct city.

Live citation status
✗ Vitals.com — Los Angeles, CA
✗ Healthgrades.com — Los Angeles, CA
✗ WebMD Care — Los Angeles, CA
✓ Doximity — Huntington Beach (correct)
✓ Health.usnews.com — Huntington Beach (correct)

Why it matters: Google cross-references NAP data across directories to verify business legitimacy. When 60% of major medical citations say "Los Angeles," Google's confidence in the Huntington Beach location drops — suppressing local pack rankings for "doctor near me" searches.

Claim and correct each profile (free, ~1 hour total per directory). Push the corrected NAP through the next 30 days and watch local pack visibility climb.
