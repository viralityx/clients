Bridge's operating rule (from Victor's notes)

AI drafts. Steph reviews. Steph sends.

For any personalized client communication — eval summaries, plan emails, 1–3 month update emails, Loom video scripts — AI does the drafting → Steph reviews → Steph clicks send. Full auto-send is reserved for transactional touches: appointment confirmations, reminders, booking links, no-show recovery, review-ask SMS. This keeps Victor's voice in the content and Steph in the loop on every client.

The journey, stage by stage

01
DAY 0 · 0–5 min

Lead captured and qualified

Phase 3 build AI-assisted

A new lead hits any of the 9 channels. Within minutes, an AI-assisted SMS thread asks two or three short questions to identify which service line they need, what their goal is, and how soon they're ready to start.

What happens
  • Lead lands in GHL contacts with a source tag (e.g., bridge fb ads, name via lookup).
  • AI SMS thread fires within 60 seconds: "Hey, this is Bridge — quick question, what are you looking to work on?"
  • Based on reply, AI assigns service-line tag automatically.
  • Missed-call leads get auto-text-back inside 60 seconds with booking link.
How it fires
  • Trigger: new GHL contact created from any source
  • AI classifies reply → adds tag from physical therapy / pitcher/thrower / recovery/arm care / strength & conditioning (bb/sb) / strength & conditioning (non-bb/sb)
  • Adds new lead tag
  • Existing "Active Tag added Workflow" auto-creates Opportunity
OutcomeEvery lead is service-line-tagged within 5 minutes. No more Steph manually typing names from referral texts. After-hours leads no longer fall through.
02
DAY 0 · 5–30 min

Auto-booked or routed to Steph

Phase 3 build

Once service line is identified, two paths: simple/clear cases auto-book a discovery call on Jane's calendar. Complex/sensitive cases (rehab post-injury, parent of injured kid) route to Steph with full context for a human conversation.

Auto-book path
  • AI sends Jane booking link tied to correct service-line calendar.
  • Client picks a slot from Discovery Call (10am–12pm / 12pm–4pm / 4pm–7pm).
  • Confirmation SMS + email fire immediately.
  • Tag flips to scheduled.
Manual path (when needed)
  • Rehab / complex cases route to Steph with summary of what AI learned.
  • Steph calls back within business hours, books on appropriate calendar.
  • Manual override always available — automation never gets in the way.
Outcome~60% of leads book themselves, freeing Steph for the 40% that need a human first. Same-day booking conversion rises from ~40% (today's manual) to ~70%.
03
DAY 1–7

Discovery call

Already exists Steph / Victor

15–30 minute free intro call. Person hears about Bridge, asks questions, gets a recommendation on which service is right for them. This is the human moment — the relationship starts here.

Pre-call automation (Phase 2)
  • Day-before SMS reminder fires automatically.
  • "What to expect" content sent based on service-line tag.
  • Calendar invite already in their inbox.
The call itself
  • Steph or Victor runs the conversation.
  • End state: book an actual eval on Jane, OR move to "not ready" tag if not the right moment.
OutcomeDiscovery call → eval conversion is the highest-leverage metric to track. Currently no instrumentation. Phase 2 dashboards will surface this.
04
DAY 7–14

Pre-eval: confirmation + intake

Phase 2 #2 entry Automated

The eval is booked in Jane. Now Phase 2 kicks in. Confirmation, intake form, and "what to expect" content all auto-deliver, ramping the client toward a high-quality first session.

What fires when
  • T+5 min after booking: Confirmation email + SMS
  • T+1 day: Intake form link sent (auto-filled with what we already know)
  • T-2 days from appt: "What to expect" content, service-specific
  • T-1 day: Reminder SMS with location + clinician name
Data we lean on
  • upcoming_appt_start_at — when
  • upcoming_appt_treatment_name — service
  • upcoming_appt_staff_member_name — clinician
  • upcoming_appt_reminder_start_time — pre-computed by Clinic Sync
Service-line branching Recovery/Rehab clients get a "what to bring (insurance, prior imaging, current pain log)" guide. Pitching/S&C clients get a "what to wear, how to warm up" guide. Same workflow, different content variants.
OutcomeNo-show rate drops significantly. Clients arrive ready, intake form already filled, mental model prepped. First session quality goes up.
05
DAY 14 (or earlier)

Initial evaluation

Phase 1 app live Clinician

Client arrives, clinician runs the service-specific battery. S&C uses Kinvent + Voltra. Pitching uses Phase 1 app + tracking software. PT uses Jane chart + recommended tests by body part / sport. All data captured into the Phase 1 database.

What happens
  • Clinician runs service-specific assessment battery.
  • Plyo / one-pager report generates as eval completes.
  • Clinician dictates raw notes → AI SOAP tool reformats to Jane structure.
  • SOAP reviewed by clinician, pasted into Jane chart.
Tools by service line
  • S&C: Kinvent + Voltra + Phase 1 app · Bridge Athletic for programming
  • Pitching: Phase 1 app + tracking / programming software
  • PT / Recovery: Jane chart + body-part-specific tests + HEP delivery
  • Deliverable #1 AI SOAP saves 10–15 min per session
OutcomeClinician finishes session admin in 5–10 minutes instead of 30. Service-specific tools stay in clinician's hands; AI handles the documentation.
06
DAY 14 · same-day

Post-eval send — AI drafts, Steph sends

Phase 2 #2 Hybrid: AI + Steph

The moment eval data lands and syncs to GHL, AI drafts the personalized client email (summary + plan + HEP for PT). Steph reviews and sends. Then SMS booking link and follow-up nudges fire fully automatic. Victor's operating rule: AI drafts personal content, Steph sends.

What fires when
  • T+0 min: AI drafts email summary + plan (+ HEP for PT) → Steph review queue
  • T+0 min auto: Branded PDF report attached
  • T+~15 min (after Steph clicks send): Email goes out
  • T+1 hr auto: SMS with follow-up booking link
  • T+24 hr auto: Nudge SMS if no follow-up booked
  • T+72 hr auto: Task to Steph if still no booking
Auto vs. human-touch
  • Auto-send: SMS reminders, booking links, transactional confirmations
  • Steph reviews: Personalized email summary, plan content, HEP details
  • Entry: service-line tag + active = Yes + arrived tag
  • Compliance: do_not_email, send_sms_notifications
Service-line variation PT clients get HEP (Home Exercise Program) attached to the email. S&C clients get a Bridge Athletic login link to access their program. Pitching gets the tracking software setup details. AI drafts each version differently.
OutcomeSame-day report delivery, but with Victor's voice preserved. Steph stays in the loop on what every client receives. Follow-up booking rate climbs because the link arrives at the emotional peak.
07
DAY 14–60+ · Block 0 forward

Active program block

Phase 2 #3 + supporting Recurring auto-touch

Client is in their program. S&C athletes are training in Bridge Athletic. Pitchers are running their throwing program. PT clients are working their HEP plus in-clinic sessions. Each appointment generates a touchpoint, each missed session generates a recovery touch.

Per-session touchpoints
  • Day before: SMS reminder with location + clinician
  • Same day: Pre-appt confirmation
  • Post-session: Optional check-in SMS (configurable)
  • If no-show: Recovery SMS within 1 hour
Data driving each fire
  • upcoming_appt_* drives all pre-session
  • last_appt_state = "no_show" drives recovery
  • no_show_count caps how often we ping chronic no-shows
Service-line variation Pitchers in-season get lighter pre-session communication (they're stressed). Off-season they get full engagement. Rehab clients get a "how are you feeling between sessions?" pulse-check that S&C clients don't.
OutcomeAttendance stays high. Drop-off gets caught at session 1–2, not session 5. Client feels seen between visits, not just during them.
08
SESSION 5

First review ask

Phase 2 #5 SMS · once only

At session 5, the client has felt the work. They've bought in. This is the highest-conversion moment to ask for a Google review. Single SMS, 24 hours after the 5th visit ends.

Trigger logic
  • Fires when appointments_count = 5
  • 24 hours after last appt end time
  • Once per client — never asked again
  • If they decline, tag added to suppress future asks
Compliance
  • Requires ratings_emails = Yes (separate from marketing opt-in)
  • SMS only — email asks underperform
  • Negative-reply detector routes to Steph if client texts back unhappy
OutcomeReview velocity 5–10x current. New clients find Bridge through fresh Google reviews. Review-driven trust compounds the ad spend.
09
EVERY 1–3 MONTHS

Retest + recurring update

Phase 2 #3 AI drafts + Steph sends

Victor's notes lock the cadence: every 1–3 months across S&C, PT, and Pitching, active clients get a recurring update with their refreshed metrics + plan. Format: email OR Loom video (Steph's call per client). AI drafts the content from latest data; Steph reviews and sends.

What fires
  • Auto-detect: client crosses 30 / 60 / 90 day mark since last retest
  • AI pulls latest metrics + drafts update (email or Loom script)
  • Steph reviews draft, edits if needed, sends as email or records as Loom
  • If retest is needed: booking link auto-included
  • Internal notification to assigned clinician (frequency change, plan update)
Service-line variation
  • S&C: 1–3 month update · Bridge Athletic data + new metrics
  • Pitcher / Thrower: 1–3 month update · velocity + KPIs per sport
  • PT / Recovery: 1–3 month update · POC progress + HEP refresh
  • Rehab (active): milestone-driven (not 1–3 month) until cleared
OutcomeThe "every 1–3 months Steph sends" cadence Victor already runs by hand becomes systematic. Retest compliance and re-engagement climb. Loom adds personal touch without manual scripting.
10
DISCHARGE / PROGRAM END

Graceful exit + second review window

Phase 2 #2 + #5

Rehab clients get discharged when they hit return-to-play. S&C and pitching clients reach program end. In both cases, the relationship doesn't end here — it transitions.

What fires
  • Discharge summary email (clinical for rehab, performance for S&C)
  • Tag added: bridge therapy past client
  • Removed from active appt reminders
  • If not yet asked: Google review SMS (review-ask fallback window)
  • 30-day post-discharge wellness check fires automatically
Second review window
  • If discharged_at populates and review wasn't asked at session 5
  • Fires 24 hrs post-discharge as a fallback
  • Same opt-in / suppression rules
OutcomeClient leaves on a high note. Their last interaction is positive, structured, and sets up the return relationship. Review velocity gets a second shot.
11
30 / 60 / 90 DAY · pack-aware

Reactivation sequence

Phase 2 #4 Pack-aware tiered SMS

Lapsed clients get a three-touch sequence with tone shifting each tier. Pack-aware: clients with sessions remaining get a warm "let's get you back in," while depleted-pack clients get an offer for a new pack. Steph only intervenes when someone replies.

The cadence
  • Day 30: Friendly check-in. No CTA. Pure relationship.
  • Day 60: Reschedule offer with booking link (or pack-renewal offer if depleted).
  • Day 90: "We miss you" — softer close, possible incentive (Victor's call).
  • Day 120+: Off active sequence, into quarterly newsletter list.
Pack-aware branching
  • Sessions remaining: "Your pack still has X sessions — let's use them"
  • Pack depleted: "Ready to grab a new pack? Here's a refresh on what's next"
  • Filter: marketing_emails = Yes, exclude bridge therapy not interested
  • LTV-aware via total_purchased
Service-line variation Rehab clients pause out at discharge — they shouldn't get "we miss you" pings unless they re-show symptoms. S&C / pitching clients have seasonal pauses (in-season vs. off-season) that can shift cadence. Victor confirms cadence later if needed.
OutcomeReactivation conversion 3–5x baseline. Steph's manual outreach drops to "respond when someone replies." Pack-renewal share of revenue grows.
12
ONGOING · post-program

Referral flywheel — 3 loops

Phase 3 build Compounds everything

The branded report from Stage 6 is Bridge's most shareable asset. Phase 3 turns it into three parallel referral engines: client-to-friend, physician-to-Bridge, and high-school-coach-to-Bridge. All three loops auto-credit attribution.

B2C client referral
  • At session 10 or discharge: branded referral link auto-sent
  • Friend books through link → both parties get notified
  • Attribution tracked back to original client
  • Referring client gets a thank-you / loyalty bump
B2B physician + coach loops
  • Physician: referrers (Fox PT, etc.) auto-receive outcome summary at discharge
  • Coach: high school coaches who refer athletes (Hannah at Gulliver pattern) receive eval results + plan summary
  • Both flagged via referring_professional + referring_staff_member_id
  • Builds Bridge's reputation in physician + sports-coach networks
OutcomeReferral attribution becomes measurable across all three loops. CAC trends down as referred leads (highest LTV) become a bigger share of the funnel. Sports-coach pipeline compounds Bridge's local reputation around schools.
