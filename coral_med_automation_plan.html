03 🚨 viralityX forms & PHI — non-negotiable
Hard rule

No BAA exists with viralityX. viralityX forms cannot be used for any patient-facing intake, registration, or anything that collects PHI.

  • New patient registration forms in viralityX
  • Any viralityX form asking for DOB, insurance, address, or medical history
  • Any viralityX survey or intake questionnaire
Form typeWhere it livesSafe?
New patient intake, demographics, medical historyPatient Ally / Intake Pro only✓ Safe
HIPAA acknowledgment, consent to treatPatient Ally / Intake Pro only✓ Safe
Insurance info, financial responsibilityPatient Ally / Intake Pro only✓ Safe
Generic "request an appointment" (name + phone only)viralityX✓ Safe
Any form with DOB, insurance, or clinical infoNever in viralityX without BAA🚨 Prohibited
Implementation note

The VA must be explicitly told: viralityX forms are for lead capture only — name and phone number. Everything clinical goes through Patient Ally. This rule must live in the VA's written SOP and onboarding checklist — not just communicated verbally. A verbal instruction will not hold.

04 Walk-in / phone patient — updated after meeting
Decision from meeting

Walk-ins at Coral Med are rare and almost always existing patients. Ameer confirmed current process is to go directly into Office Ally and add them to the schedule. The viralityX-first SOP was designed for new patients — since walk-ins are almost never new, this rule is significantly simplified.

Path of least resistance — Ameer's requirement

Ameer explicitly flagged he does not want extra steps. The VA should not have to open two systems for existing patients. viralityX-first only applies to genuinely new patients entering the practice for the first time.

07 Copay collection — confirmed workflow
Confirmed by Dr. Moussa

Copay is collected at time of appointment via cash or credit card. Eligibility, copay amount, and any outstanding balance must be confirmed 24–48 hours before the appointment so the front desk knows exactly what to collect when the patient arrives — no surprises at checkout.

Pre-visit copay workflow — confirmed

StepWhoWhenHow
Check eligibility + confirm copay + any outstanding balance👤 VA / Front office24–48 hrs before apptMedi-Cal portal / Medicare Advantage plan portal
Enter confirmed copay amount in Office Ally👤 VA / Front officeSame time as eligibility checkOffice Ally UI — "copay due" field
Send pre-visit reminder SMS with copay amount⚡ viralityX24 hrs before apptAutomated SMS — e.g. "Reminder: your appt is tomorrow at 2pm. Your copay is $25. Please have cash or card ready."
Collect copay at appointment👤 Front officeAt time of appt — cash or CCFront desk sees confirmed copay in Office Ally, collects before patient leaves
Follow up on remaining balance👤 Front office / VAAfter insurance adjudicates claimManual call or message — e.g. if deductible applies beyond the copay collected
What viralityX automates in this workflow
The pre-visit SMS reminder with the confirmed copay amount. This sets the patient's expectation before they arrive, eliminates checkout friction, and reduces the chance of patients being caught off guard. The VA must enter the copay into Office Ally during the 24–48 hr eligibility check for the automation to pull the correct amount.
SOP requirement

The 24–48 hr eligibility + copay check must be a formal VA task — not optional. If it's missed, the reminder SMS either sends without an amount or sends incorrect information. This needs to be in the VA's daily task checklist for every appointment on the next day's schedule.
