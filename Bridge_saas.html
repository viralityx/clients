01 · THE PROBLEM

Four pains. One software gap.

Victor handed us the entire diagnosis on the discovery calls. Every clinic Chris trains hits the same wall — and the wall is software, not hardware.

01
The mental-math moment
Therapist tests injured side, then healthy side, then squints at a calculator while the patient watches. The "objective" test becomes a fumble. The trust hit happens in real time.
"They're doing math in front of the patient. It looks like a dinosaur."
02
Nothing goes home
No PDF. No email. No way for a parent or referring physician to see progress between sessions. The session ends, the data dies on a Post-it.
03
EMRs ignore strength
WebPT and Jane handle scheduling and notes but have zero strength tracking. Clinics that bought the $250 device have nowhere to put the numbers it produces.
04
Competitor norms are polluted
Kinvent and Vald average injured + healthy + all ages together — useless benchmarks. Vald is enterprise-priced. Kinvent moves slow and big-company. Excel sheets are what's left.
02 · LOCKED DECISIONS

Three constraints. Everything else flows from these.

Each decision below kills a hundred feature debates downstream. Hold the line on these and the MVP almost designs itself.

Decision 01 · ICP Wedge

Mid-size hybrid clinics — 4 to 15 therapists.

We sell first into clinics that look like Bridge Water. Hybrid performance + rehab, multiple staff, admin who signs the contract, clinicians who use the product daily. ACV target: $300–800/mo. We earn the right to drop a Solo PT tier ($79/mo) once the workflow is proven.
Why this and not solo PT first. Distribution. Victor and Chris are already in this segment — their workshops, their network, their FIU students. Solo PT is the bigger market on paper but cold without a wedge. We're not building broad, we're building deep where the demand is already warm.
Decision 02 · Wow Moment

Side-to-side gap closing + auto-emailed PDF.

Every PT case has a healthy side and an injured side. The universal question across ACL, rotator cuff, ankle, hip — every diagnosis — is are we closing the gap? That visualization is the hero. The auto-emailed report is the close.
Why not radar / norms / AI insights first. Those are second-test value. They require cohort data we don't have day one and a learning curve to interpret. The gap chart works on the very first session — therapist tests both sides, screen shows the delta, parent gets a PDF before they leave the parking lot. That's the demo we win on.
Decision 03 · Build Approach

Hybrid — fresh multi-tenant shell, port the 22 formulas.

Don't fork Victor's Replit build. Nikki architects fresh: auth, tenancy, billing, RBAC from clean ground. Then we port the formula library (Z-Score, Torque, FBW/TBW, DSI, LSI, RTD, RFD/BW, Sorensen + the rest) and the dark-premium report engine. That's where the real IP lives.
Why not just fork. Bridge Water's app has clinic-specific assumptions baked into every model — service types, role definitions, the column shapes of their old Excel sheets. Forking means dragging those assumptions into every future tenant. ~10 weeks to a clean MVP is worth more than ~6 weeks to a messy one we can't sell.
03 · MVP SCOPE

What we keep, strip, and build new.

Bridge Water's build was a custom platform with three intake types and 22 formulas. The SaaS strips it back to one job: test, see the gap, send the report.

Keep
From Bridge Water, port directly
Formula library (22 calcs)
Z-Score, Torque, FBW/TBW, DSI, LSI, RTD, RFD/BW, Sorensen, etc. This is the IP.
Dark-premium PDF report engine
Top-strength callout, trend arrows, tier badges. Already gorgeous — reuse the templates.
Approval queue
Clinician reviews before patient gets email. Liability + branding moat. Keep.
Admin vs staff permissions
Staff edits metrics, admin owns delete/export/billing. Already proven model.
DOB auto age calc
Tiny but Victor specifically asked for this. Costs nothing, signals craft.
Most-recent-test auto-pull
Critical for longitudinal view. Already solved.
Strip
Out of MVP — re-add later if asked
3 service types (THROWERS / PLYO / PATIENT)
SaaS day 1 = one universal intake. Service types are a Bridge Water org-shape.
303-column PLYO intake
Day 1 schema is small and opinionated. Power users get custom fields later.
20-sheet PATIENT structure
Same — model after universal session, not Bridge Water's spreadsheet legacy.
Compare athlete vs specific other athlete
Cool but niche. v1.1 feature. Cohort comparison is enough at MVP.
Editable Canva-style report toggle
Defer. Ship one great default report. Editor adds weeks of UX.
Norm playground (live override)
Brilliant Victor idea but advanced UX. v1.1.
New
SaaS-only — not in Bridge Water build
Multi-tenant architecture
Every clinic isolated. Row-level security from day one. This is the SaaS unlock.
Self-serve clinic signup
Sign up → enter clinic name → 15-min onboarding → first test. No Nikki in the loop.
Stripe billing + trial
14-day free trial, card up front. Tier change in-app. Annual discount.
Seedable norms with auto-evolve
Admin pastes published norms. As clinic logs sessions, system blends and labels "manual" vs "calculated".
Side-to-side gap engine
First-class chart, not buried. The hero feature on the dashboard.
Parent / referring-doc email link
Tokenized URL to view PDF without an account. Conversion engine — every patient sees the brand.

Nikki architects the foundation first. We don't port a single Bridge Water formula until tenancy and billing are clean.

  • Feedback loop → v1.0 lock

07 · NEW CLINIC ONBOARDING

From signup to first test — under 15 minutes.

If a clinic can't get from I signed up to I just sent a parent a PDF on day one, we lose them. The wizard is the product as much as the dashboard is.

Min 0–2
Sign up + clinic profile
Email, clinic name, address, logo upload. Pick brand color. Stripe card on file. 14-day trial active.
Min 2–5
Invite team
Add up to 10 emails with admin/staff role. Skip-able — you can do this later. Defaults to "just me" for solo.
Min 5–8
Pick your norms starting point
Three choices: (a) start with our published seed norms, (b) paste in your own published norms, (c) start blank and build as you log. Most pick (a).
Min 8–11
Add first patient
Guided form. Name, DOB, injured body part, side, days since onset. System creates patient profile + opens first session.
Min 11–14
Log first session
Therapist enters healthy + injured side values. Gap chart appears. PDF previews. Tap approve.
Min 14–15
First PDF sent
Patient gets the email. Clinic admin gets a "you just sent your first Bridge report" confirmation. Activation event fires. They're a customer.
08 · OPEN QUESTIONS FOR VICTOR

Calls I'd rather Victor make than guess on. His answers shape the next two phases.

Strategic Partner Terms
What does "strategic partner" mean in writing?
Equity % vs revenue share vs intro commission? Is he in the cap table or a referral partner? Needs to be papered before pilots go live.
Pilot Clinics
Which 2–3 clinics in his + Chris's network are warmest for the pilot?
We need real users for weeks 9–10. Free pilot in exchange for feedback + testimonial. Victor names the list.
Norm Seeds
Which published norm sets do we ship with at launch?
Chris is the source for this. Likely shoulder ER/IR, ACL LSI, knee extension torque, grip. The "starter pack" of seed norms decides what tests we support on day 1.
Device Integration
Manual entry only at MVP, or do we wire up to the $250 device?
Manual entry is faster to ship. Direct device sync is a wow upgrade but adds 3–4 weeks. Lean: manual at MVP, device sync as a v1.1 feature once we know which device wins.
Bridge Water as Lighthouse
Is Bridge Water the first paying tenant on the SaaS, or do they stay on the custom build?
Big call. Either we migrate them (Bridge Water = case study #1) or they keep their bespoke app and we sell to peer clinics. Both viable. Affects how we shape Phase 2.
Name + Brand
Does Victor want "Bridge" the brand on the SaaS, or do we name it separately?
"Bridge" is his clinic. Using it for the SaaS is a free distribution boost but ties his clinic brand to a product. Could be a feature ("by Bridge Water Performance") or risk. Needs his nod.
